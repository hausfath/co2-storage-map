Global CO₂ Storage Atlas

This tool highlights where the world can store CO₂ — through injection into sedimentary reservoirs and by in-situ mineralization (ISM) in reactive rock. The ideal storage site depends on transport distance and cost from the CO₂ source, permitting and development time, and cost per ton stored. Injection storage will likely remain cheaper, but in many parts of the world it isn't an option — and ISM is. Carbon removal at scale needs a diversity of storage sites around the globe.
