# CO2 Geologic Storage Capacity — United States and Canada (tiered)

*Compiled by research agent, 2026-07-13. Source workstream: sedimentary capacity R1.*

**Critical framing:** USGS (2013), NETL/NATCARB Atlas, and Teletzke et al. (2018) US figures
are NOT additive — they are progressively more conservative re-filterings of the same
resource base (theoretical/technical → practicable). Never sum them.

## US tier ladder

| Source | Year | Tier | US Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| USGS National Assessment | 2013 | Technical (TASR) | ~3,000 Gt (2,400–3,700 P95/P05) |
| NETL/NATCARB Atlas IV | 2012 | Theoretical/prospective | ≥2,400 Gt |
| NETL/NATCARB Atlas V | 2015 | Theoretical/prospective | ~2,600 Gt |
| Teletzke et al. (ExxonMobil, GHGT-14) | 2018 | Practicable | 506 Gt (P50) / 406 Gt (P95) |

### USGS 2013 (Technical, TASR)
- 36 onshore/state-waters sedimentary basins, formations ≥3,000 ft depth, probabilistic.
- Coastal Plains region ~2,000 Gt = ~65% of national total; Gulf Coast alone = 59%.
- Remaining basins (Colorado Plateau, Rockies, Midwest, etc.) ≈ 1,000 Gt.
- Sources: https://pubs.usgs.gov/fs/2013/3020/ ; https://pubs.usgs.gov/circ/1386/

### NETL/NATCARB Atlas
- Atlas IV (2012): ≥2,400 Gt across saline + O&G + coal; depleted O&G >225 Gt.
- Atlas V (2015): ~2,600 Gt; O&G fields ~230 Gt. Full basin tables in ATLAS-V-2015.pdf
  (55 MB, https://www.netl.doe.gov/node/5841); EDX: https://edx.netl.doe.gov/dataset/netl-carbon-storage-atlas-fifth-edition
- The oft-quoted "2,379–21,633 Gt" spread could NOT be confirmed against Atlas IV/V editions.

### Teletzke et al. 2018 (Practicable)
- 506 Gt P50 / 406 Gt P95, saline only, onshore; ~8–17% retention of USGS technical base.
- Basin breakdown (via secondary citation, PMC10569028 / ACS EST 10.1021/acs.est.3c00790):
  Gulf Coast 366 Gt (>72%); California 30 Gt; North Dakota/Williston 15 Gt;
  Illinois-Basin five-state area 12 Gt; other basins ~83 Gt.
- Primary (SSRN 3366176) was 403-blocked; figures corroborated via peer-reviewed secondary.

## Canada
- National theoretical/technical (51 basins; provisional — secondary summary of
  ScienceDirect S0959652623010594): saline 778 Gt; gas fields 91 Gt; oil fields 8 Gt;
  total ≈ 877 Gt. Verify against Bachu primary literature before treating as canonical.
- WCSB (Alberta + Williston): ~385–390 Gt theoretical/technical (Intl CCS Knowledge
  Centre, "Canada's CO2 Landscape" Apr 2021).
- Practical-tier illustrations (Bachu & Shaw, W. Canada): gas reservoirs 3.2 Gt;
  oil reservoirs 0.56 Gt. Athabasca Devonian saline prospects ~4 Gt.
- CSRC Cycle 4/5 Canada: ~148 Gt aggregated (stricter maturity criteria).

## Gulf Coast dominance
Gulf Coast dominates every US tier: 59% of USGS TASR; 72% (366 Gt) of practicable.
